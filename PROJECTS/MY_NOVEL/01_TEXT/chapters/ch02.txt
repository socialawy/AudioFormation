The morning came slowly, as mornings often do in the desert. The sun crept over the dunes like a thief, stealing the darkness inch by inch.

She stood at the edge of the cliff, watching the world wake up beneath her. There was something about this place that made everything else seem small and far away.

"We should go," he said, his voice barely above a whisper. But she didn't move. Not yet. Not until the light reached the valley floor.
